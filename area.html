Guide

How this conversion works

Area conversions run through square meters as the base unit. Because area is a squared dimension, doubling a length unit quadruples the corresponding area — for example 1 square yard is 9 square feet, not 3, since a yard is 3 feet on each side.

Hectares and acres are the two units most often used for land size: a hectare is exactly 10,000 m², while an acre is defined as 43,560 square feet, which works out to roughly 0.405 hectares.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How big is an acre, roughly?

About 4,047 square meters, or close to the size of a standard American football field without the end zones. It's also roughly 0.405 hectares.

Why isn't 1 square yard equal to 3 square feet?

Because area scales with the square of length. A yard is 3 feet, so a square yard (3 ft × 3 ft) is 9 square feet, not 3. This trips people up more often with area than with any other unit type.
